I am Jakub Bajla. I help enterprises adopt Microsoft AI without losing control of it: the governance, the architecture, and the discipline to take agentic AI from a promising pilot to something you can actually run.

I am a Power Platform and AI architect, a Microsoft MVP for Business Applications, the first in Slovakia, and I lead the Power Platform and AI competency at EY. My background spans Microsoft, Microsoft partners, and customer organizations, so I have seen adoption from every angle.

My work sits where most AI programs struggle. Not the demo, but the operating model, the architecture, and the governance that decide whether adoption sticks. I work process-first, and I prove patterns by building them rather than leaving them on paper. I created and teach the Governance Compass, a practical operating model that treats Microsoft 365 Copilot, Copilot Studio agents, and the Power Platform as one coherent system, not three disconnected tools.

What I believe: the model is a commodity you rent, and the value is in the implementation you own. The edge in agentic AI is not years of tool experience, which no one has yet, but enterprise-architecture discipline applied to immature technology: auditing an early build, correcting the design, and keeping every decision traceable to intent.

This site is the technical half of that work: reference architectures, governance patterns, and honest notes from real builds, including the parts that broke. Shorter takes go out on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jakubbajla/).

Enterprise architecture for the agentic era.
